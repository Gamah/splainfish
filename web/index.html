splainfish

Most engines tell you how much a move changed the evaluation. splainfish explains why: it runs Stockfish's NNUE forward pass on both positions, diffs the network activations layer by layer, and back-projects the output change onto the chess concepts that changed — a pawn shield, an enemy knight reaching your king's zone, a rook finding an open file.

Evaluations and move quality come from Stockfish. The explanations are derived deterministically from the NNUE activations — no language model involved. The attribution shows relative direction and emphasis, not calibrated centipawns.

Curious what Best, Inaccuracy, or Blunder actually mean, and how the thresholds are set? See the move-quality labels reference.
